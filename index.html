Medieval Weapons

Jousting

A common practice found in Medieval times was the game of Jousting. Not being part of the main event of a tournament, a Joust was often the prelude to the main event. Two people mounted horses and rode them towards each other. They used laces, which were often 10 feet in length, to knock the opponent off the horse.

Pike

The Pike was a weapon found in the middle and late medieval period in Europe. Avergaing in about 20 feet in length, the pike was handled with two hands and required high amounts of strength to wield. The length of the Pike was proven as advantageous because it allowed the holder to strike from a safer distance. At the tip, a strong metal layed sturdy for combat pruposes.

Mace

The mace was a popular weapon that was often made out of wood or a strong metal. The long shaft that the person would use was made up of wood and the ball at the end could differ. Instead of being spiked, it could be sphered and curved without any spikes. The mace was a symbol of Medieval knights and were often used in tournaments because of its small weight.

Catapult

The catapult during the medieval period was a important weapon. Originating from greek roots, its name meant "Shield Piercing" to to symbolize the strength that catapult had. Catapults during the medieval period saw minimal uses because of the quick introduction to gunpoweder and new technology. Catapult originated from crossbows and were used to throw rocks or other payloads at walls or even armies.
